Welcome to Bihar Tourism

Discover the cultural richness and natural beauty of Bihar. From its bustling cities to tranquil wilderness, Bihar has something for everyone.

Top Destinations

Gaya

Mahabodhi Temple

Gaya is an important religious destination in Bihar. It is home to the Mahabodhi Temple, where Lord Buddha attained enlightenment.

Nalanda

Vishwa Shanti Stupa

Nalanda is a historical city, home to the ancient Nalanda University and the Vishwa Shanti Stupa.

Vaishali

Ashokan Pillar

Vaishali is a significant archaeological site in Bihar, known for the Ashokan Pillar and its connection to Lord Buddha's teachings.

Patna

Gol Ghar

Patna, the capital city of Bihar, is known for historical landmarks like Gol Ghar, a large granary built by the British in 1786.

Art & Culture of Bihar

Maithili Painting

Traditional folk art of Bihar, known for intricate patterns and vibrant colors.

Maithili painting is a famous form of folk art from the Mithila region of Bihar, characterized by vibrant colors and detailed patterns.

Sikki Art

Handicraft art made using grass, intricately woven into beautiful patterns.

Sikki art is a traditional craft of Bihar, where the grass is woven into decorative pieces such as baskets and dolls.

Bihar Handloom

Traditional handwoven textiles from Bihar, including Madhubani sarees and other fabrics.

Bihar's handloom industry is known for its exquisite Madhubani sarees and other traditional textiles, which are made using ancient weaving techniques passed down through generations.

Extraordinary Places to Visit

Kesaria Stupa

The tallest Buddhist stupa in the world.

This grand stupa is believed to be built by Emperor Ashoka and holds great historical importance.

Rohtasgarh Fort

A historical fort known for its magnificent architecture.

Built by Sher Shah Suri, this fort showcases medieval Indian military architecture.

Mandar Hill

A mythological and historical hill associated with the churning of the ocean (Samudra Manthan).

Mandar Hill in Banka district is a sacred and scenic spot linked to Hindu mythology, attracting pilgrims and trekkers alike.

Kanwar Lake

The largest freshwater oxbow lake in India, a paradise for bird watchers.

Kanwar Lake in Begusarai is Asia's largest oxbow lake, attracting thousands of migratory birds and offering stunning natural beauty.

Famous Festivals

Chhath Puja

A major festival dedicated to the Sun God, celebrated with devotion across Bihar.

Chhath Puja is an important festival in Bihar where devotees offer prayers to the Sun God for health, wealth, and prosperity.

Buddha Purnima

Celebrates the birth, enlightenment, and death anniversary of Lord Buddha.

Buddha Purnima marks the anniversary of Lord Buddha's birth and enlightenment. It is observed in Bodh Gaya with prayers and rituals.

Makar Sankranti

A festival celebrating the harvest season and the arrival of winter.

Makar Sankranti is celebrated in Bihar with kite flying, bonfires, and special foods, marking the harvest season.

Holi

Celebration of colors, joy, and the arrival of spring.

Holi in Bihar is celebrated with a unique fervor, with people playing with colors, dancing, and enjoying delicious sweets.
